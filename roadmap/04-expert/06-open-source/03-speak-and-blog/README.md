# Speak / Blog

## Purpose

This topic is about sharing engineering knowledge publicly through talks and writing in a way that is technically credible, useful to practitioners, and aligned with real production lessons.

## Enterprise-Level Pointers

- why public technical communication matters
- turning production lessons into durable public artifacts
- structuring talks and articles around engineering clarity
- balancing depth, accuracy, and accessibility
- protecting confidential or sensitive information
- credibility through evidence and concrete examples
- maintaining a public technical voice over time
- audience-focused communication for engineers
- review and publication quality standards
- common production mistakes
- maintainability rules
- interview-style questions
- practice exercises

## What Expert Teams Optimize For

- sharing knowledge that helps other engineers make better decisions
- building technical influence through clarity and rigor
- reusing internal lessons without leaking sensitive context
- creating public material that remains useful after the event or release date

## Common Production Mistakes

- publishing vague inspiration instead of concrete engineering content
- exaggerating scale or lessons without evidence
- leaking confidential implementation or customer details
- writing content that cannot survive basic technical review

## Maintainability Rules

- anchor talks and blogs in real engineering decisions and evidence
- remove sensitive details while keeping the technical lesson intact
- prefer clarity and structure over hype
- review public content with the same rigor as important documentation

## Interview Questions

- Why is public technical writing part of engineering leadership?
- How do you share lessons from production systems safely?
- What makes a technical talk or article credible to experienced engineers?

## Practice Exercises

- Outline a conference talk based on a production incident lesson.
- Turn an internal design decision into a public blog structure.
- Create a review checklist for public technical content.
