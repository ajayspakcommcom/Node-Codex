# Mentoring Juniors

## Purpose

Mentoring juniors is a senior engineering responsibility because strong teams scale through better judgment, stronger independence, and shared technical understanding rather than through senior engineers doing all difficult work themselves.

## Enterprise-Level Pointers

- mentoring as capability building, not answer delivery
- teaching tradeoff reasoning instead of prescribing solutions only
- calibrating feedback to experience level and task risk
- giving context around codebase, operations, and quality expectations
- helping juniors learn debugging and review skills
- creating safe escalation paths without creating dependency
- balancing support with accountability
- using code reviews, pairing, and documentation as mentoring tools
- identifying growth areas and setting learning goals
- mentoring during incidents and production work carefully
- common production mistakes
- maintainability rules
- interview-style questions
- practice exercises

## What Enterprise Teams Optimize For

- stronger independent contributors over time
- lower knowledge concentration in a few senior people
- safer junior participation in important systems
- sustained growth in team judgment and confidence

## Common Production Mistakes

- solving every problem for the junior engineer
- giving feedback without enough context to learn from it
- delegating critical work without proper support or guardrails
- confusing mentoring with permanent hand-holding
- failing to explain why a decision matters in production systems

## Maintainability Rules

- mentor toward independent reasoning, not dependency on approval
- explain tradeoffs and system consequences clearly
- give feedback that is specific, timely, and growth-oriented
- use mentoring to spread operational and architectural understanding
